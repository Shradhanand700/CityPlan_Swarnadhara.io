1. Green Energy & Ocean Energy Hub

Description

A hub that develops, tests, and deploys offshore wind, tidal, wave, and OTEC energy, with storage, grid integration, port-side manufacturing, and workforce training.

Economic Impact
  • Creates high‑skill engineering and marine jobs.
  • Improves energy security and reduces fuel imports.
  • Builds local supply chains around ports and cabling.
  • Enables export of design, installation, and O&M services.

2. Clean Water & Environmental Industries

Description

Enterprises providing purification, desalination, wastewater reuse, pollution control, and environmental monitoring for secure, healthy water systems.

Economic Impact
  • Secures water for households, farms, and industry.
  • Reduces health burden from water‑borne diseases.
  • Creates engineering and operations jobs.
  • Enables industry growth wherever water is reliable.

SDG 6 SDG 3 SDG 11 SDG 12 SDG 14

Description

Integrated transport: electric buses and ferries, smart public transit, intermodal freight, green last‑mile delivery, and port logistics on digital platforms.

Economic Impact
  • Lowers logistics costs and boosts competitiveness.
  • Creates jobs in fleets, warehousing, and IT.
  • Reduces congestion and time lost in traffic.
  • Improves trade via better port connectivity.

Description

Shipyards and workshops that design, build, retrofit, and repair vessels, from fishing boats to commercial ships and offshore platforms.

Economic Impact
  • Provides large numbers of skilled and semi‑skilled jobs.
  • Builds maritime and naval industrial capacity.

Convert coastal waste (seaweed, biomass, fishing residues, plastics) into fuels and power via biochemical, thermochemical, and advanced recycling.

Course Reflections – Design & Analysis of Algorithms

Key conceptual takeaways from iteration, recursion, efficiency, hierarchies, graphs, and major algorithmic techniques.

1. Problems in Nature – Iteration, Recursion, Backtracking

Nature exhibits repeated cycles (seasons), self-similar structures, and trial‑and‑error exploration that mirror iteration, recursion, and backtracking patterns.

2. Space & Time Efficiency – Orders of Growth

Space efficiency is extra memory an algorithm needs; time efficiency is how long it runs. Both matter for scalability and cost.

Common classes: O(1), O(log n), O(n), O(n log n), O(n²), O(2ⁿ), O(n!).

3. Design Principles (Brave vs Cautious, Prefix/Suffix, Balance)

Traversal metaphors (brave vs cautious) connect to BFS/DFS; prefix/suffix reasoning drives efficient string algorithms; balancing keeps trees efficient under updates.

4. Hierarchical Data – Trees & Variants

BSTs, AVL, 2–3 trees, red‑black trees, heaps, and tries show trade‑offs in search speed, balancing cost, and usage (priority queues, dictionaries, indexes).

5. Array Query Algorithms

Range queries and updates (Fenwick tree, segment tree, precomputed tables) underpin fast analytics in finance, e‑commerce, and logs.

6. Trees vs Graphs & Traversals

Trees are connected, acyclic, with n–1 edges; graphs are general. Traversals (BFS/DFS) enable routing, connectivity, and exploration in graphs; tree traversals power BST operations and expression evaluation.

7. Sorting & Searching Connections

Simple sorts (bubble, selection, insertion) are educational; merge and quick sort deliver O(n log n) performance. Searching spans linear, binary, and graph searches.

8. Graph Algorithms – Spanning Trees & Shortest Paths

Spanning trees minimize total connection cost; shortest paths minimize travel time or cost, foundational for networking and routing.

9. Algorithm Design Techniques

Backtracking, divide & conquer, shortest path algorithms, and graph traversals form a toolkit for search, optimization, and exploration problems.
